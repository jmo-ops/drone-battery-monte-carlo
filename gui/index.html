    GUI

    The project includes a desktop GUI (Tkinter + matplotlib) that lets you run Monte Carlo simulations and compare two battery configurations across typical and extreme mission conditions.

    Extreme Conditions tab

    Tabs and features

    1) Simulation

    Run the standard Monte Carlo simulation to compare Battery A vs Battery B under nominal sampling assumptions. The GUI produces:

    • Text summary of results (success rate, remaining energy stats, operational averages)
    • Distribution plots (remaining energy histograms)

    2) Parameters Summary

    Shows the active parameters and model settings used during the run (battery parameters, mission config, penalties, and sampling ranges).

    3) Extreme Conditions

    Runs a deliberately biased Monte Carlo intended to stress the mission (high wind, low temperature, high payload, and slightly longer distance).

    Extreme simulation settings

    • Number of runs per battery (example shown: 500)
    • Biases:
    • Wind: high-biased (10–20 mph)
    • Temp: low-biased (10–95 °F)
    • Payload: high-biased (2.5–5 lb)
    • Distance factor: high-biased (1.0–1.1)
